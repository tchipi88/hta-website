The Challenge

The World Health Organization (WHO) has highlighted that the “prevention and control of chronic diseases is a major development imperative for the 21st century”.

Cardiovascular disease is the second leading cause of death in sub-Saharan Africa and is the first among those over the age of 30.

1 in 3 people in Africa has high blood pressure but only 7% are controlled. High blood pressure is a major contributor to cardiovascular complications and death.

GANEO-app combines evidence-based clinical programs, advanced analytics and human coaching to help people to reduce their blood pressure and their heart risk and improve their health.

Our Solution

Connecting patients, health workers and physician to improve chronic diseases prevention in Africa

Ganeo-app is redesigning the management of individuals with chronic disease in Africa by accelerating their contact with health care providers, facilitating the implementation of lifestyle change and monitoring their health-related data to share it with their clinician.

How we do it

Home based screening to facilitate early intervention and access to clinicians for patients at risk

We use an evidence-based algorithm to facilitate screening, identification and referral of patients who are at risk to the health center.

Personalized health coaching and monitoring to drive behavior change

We know a one-size fits all approach doesn't work. Everyone has unique situation. GANEO-App empowers health workers to provide personalized care adapted to patients’ needs. Health workers work with patient to set personalized goal, create personalized action plan for healthy habits, provide encouragement and keep patient on track for long-term success. Data collected during home based monitoring are shared to your physician.

Evidence-based curricula to improve understanding of disease and self care

Ganeo-App embedders a broad portfolio of clinical modules and videos, developed based on evidence-based care standards and African experts to enable health workers and patients to learn about their disease and to help to build and maintain healthy habits.
